Machine Learning Analysis:

Last week we updated our dataset to include genres of all of the top songs. Once our dataset was complete with information regarding song duration on the top charts, peak performance, genre, ext. we were ready to perform analysis on our data set.

After assessing various potential options we decided to implement a naive bayes ML analysis on the dataset. For each song we added a tag that specified if it was a one-hit wonder with true representing a one-hit wonder and false representing a song that was not. This would be used as a label in our training set to learn what attributes were responsible for a one hit wonder. For features we extracted various descriptive qualities such as peak performance, time on the top charts, and genre. After storing two arrays with our training attributes and training labels, we initialized our classifier. Using the classifier we fit it with our data and were able to get the mean and std deviation scores.

Once we trained our data using song data from various years we moved onto testing it. First, we obtained new label and attribute data from our testing data. Using this new data and our classifier we used the predict function to try and estimate future labels. The next thing we hope to look into is the impact of genres the of other popular songs during the rise of a one-hit wonder. This will be an interesting way to easily visualize how specific one-hit wonder songs rise and fall over time. We may also include the one-hit wonder genre and most popular genres of the time period to visualize the impact that genres have on one-hit wonder trajectories. This is a long term goal of ours to enable users to easily visualize the path of all the one-hit wonders from the past 15 years.
